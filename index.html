    A · Alert-Response Decision Tree
    WHEN ALERT FIRES

    A TOS alert just fired. Walk this tree. If anything fails, abort.

    1
    Read the alert message. It now contains: setup name, ticker, IVR, RVOL, strength score. If any of those are red on their face (IVR over 70, RVOL under 1, strength under 3), stop here — don't even open the chart.
    2
    Confirm regime. Open the SPY chart. Read the MAC_MacroRegime label. RISK-OFF blocks longs (puts only at quarter size). NEUTRAL = half size. RISK-ON = full size.
    3
    Confirm sector. Glance at sector status mini-bar in cockpit (set Sundays). If ticker's sector is RED, only trade short setups.
    4
    Open the ticker chart in TOS. Verify: setup label matches alert, RSMK column is green or rising, RVOL is 1.5 or higher, ATR percent in normal range (2 to 4).
    5
    Look up Seeking Alpha. Quant 3.5 or higher (longs) or 2.5 or lower (puts). Profitability B- or better. Momentum B- or better. Earnings more than 7 days away.
    6
    Paste into cockpit. Use the Smart Paste field at top of Swing tab. Verify the snapshot panel shows what you expected. Fill any missing fields manually.
    7
    Walk the 5 steps. Cockpit locks each until prerequisites pass. If any gate stays red, you have your answer — don't take the trade.
    GO
    Place order in TOS. Bracket OCO: limit entry, profit target (+50% premium), stop trigger (close below invalidation). Then click GO in cockpit to log the trade.

    Your Sizing Rules
    SETTINGS
    Edit via gear icon ⚙ in top nav
    IV Rank → What to buy
    CHEAT
    IV Rank (0–100) tells you whether option prices are cheap or expensive right now. Look it up in TOS → Trade tab → ticker → Volatility section.
    • under 30 Buy a single call/put (in-the-money, 0.65–0.75 delta, 30–45 days to expiration). Options are cheap — get max leverage.
    • 30 – 50 Use a debit spread (buy one, sell one further out). Cuts your cost in half and limits your risk.
    • 50 – 70 Spread + cut size in HALF. Options are expensive — extra caution required.
    • over 70 Skip the trade. Options are too expensive — even if you\'re right on direction, the volatility crash will likely kill your gains.
    Delta = how much the option price moves for each $1 the stock moves (0.70 delta = ~70¢). Higher delta = more like owning the stock.
    Top 6 Don'ts
    DANGER
    • 01Don\'t size up after a loss. Revenge trades blow accounts. Take the same size or smaller.
    • 02Don\'t move your stop loss. If the stock hits your stop, exit. Period. "Just one more day" is how small losses become huge.
    • 03Don\'t hold long options through earnings. Volatility crashes after the report — usually wipes out gains even when you\'re right.
    • 04Don\'t trade illiquid options. If the bid-ask spread is more than 5% of the option\'s price, the market makers will eat your edge.
    • 05Don\'t buy more of a losing position. Averaging down works for stocks, not options — your time decay accelerates.
    • 06Don\'t hold options past 21 days to expiration. Time decay (theta) accelerates rapidly in the final 3 weeks. Take profit or cut losses earlier.
    Kill Switches — when to stop
    STOP
    Hard rules that override everything else. If any trigger, follow the rule — no exceptions.
    Account down 7%+ this month → stop trading for one week, review log, find the leak
    3 losing weeks in a row → switch to paper trading until you string together 5 paper wins
    Win rate under 45% over your last 20 trades → process is drifting; review every recent trade for pattern violations
    VIX spikes 30%+ in one session → no new entries that day; markets are panicking
    2 losses in the same trading day → done for the day; close laptop, go for a walk

      The 5 Approved Setups
      ONLY THESE
      If the chart doesn\'t fit one of these 5 patterns, don\'t trade it. EMA = exponential moving average (a smoothed price line).
      • 0121-EMA Pullback — Stock in uptrend dips to its 21-day moving average, then prints a bullish reversal bar (your entry signal)
      • 02Base Breakout — Stock has been flat in a tight range for 15+ days, then breaks above on 1.5× normal volume
      • 03Breakout Retest — After a breakout, stock pulls back to test the breakout level (now support), bounces — confirming the move was real
      • 049-EMA Reclaim — Stock dips below the 9-day moving average intraday, then closes back above — showing dip-buyers stepped in
      • 05Edge Reversal — Counter-trend trade after a long extended move shows exhaustion. Use HALF size; lower probability than the others
      Your TOS Studies — what each one does
      TOS
      These are the three custom thinkScript studies the cockpit references. Each lives on a chart in Thinkorswim and tells you something specific. If a study isn't loaded, right-click any chart → Studies → Edit Studies → Import to add it.
      📊 MAC_MacroRegime
      Add to: SPY daily chart (top-left label)
      What it shows
      A single label on your SPY chart that reads RISK-ON, NEUTRAL, or RISK-OFF. Looks at SPY trend, VIX level, and breadth to decide which environment you're in.
      What to look for
      RISK-ON = full size long trades allowed. NEUTRAL = half size, both directions. RISK-OFF = reduced size, longs blocked, puts only on weak sectors.
      How to use it
      Glance at SPY chart every Sunday. Click the matching button at the top right of this cockpit (RISK-ON / NEUTRAL / RISK-OFF). Sets your sizing for the week.
      🚦 MAC_AllSetups_Dashboard
      Add to: every ticker in your weekly watchlist
      What it shows
      A composite signal that fires when ANY of your 5 approved setups triggers (21-EMA Pullback, Base Breakout, Breakout Retest, 9-EMA Reclaim, or Edge Reversal). The Signal output is a number between 0 and 1 — anything above 0.5 means a setup just printed.
      What to look for
      A green or amber arrow appears on the bar where the setup printed. The label shows which of the 5 setups triggered (e.g. "21-EMA PULLBACK").
      How to use it
      Set an alert: right-click chart → Create Alert → Study condition → MAC_AllSetups_Dashboard.Signal is greater than 0.5 (Daily timeframe). When the alert fires, open the chart, verify the setup matches one of the 5 patterns, and run through the 5 cockpit steps.
      ↩️ MAC_Setup_EdgeReversal
      Add to: tickers you've been tracking for extended trends
      What it shows
      A specialized study just for setup #5 (Edge Reversal). Detects exhaustion bars after a long extended move — gap-up after weeks of rally, or gap-down after extended decline. Signal value not equal to 0 means an exhaustion bar printed.
      What to look for
      Positive signal value = exhaustion of an UPtrend (potential short / put play). Negative signal value = exhaustion of a DOWNtrend (potential long / call play).
      How to use it
      Optional second alert layer. Edge Reversal is the lowest-probability of your 5 setups, so you trade it at HALF size. If you want extra confirmation, add this study and set an alert when its Signal is not equal to 0.
      Tip: Save these three studies as a Chart Style in TOS so they auto-load on every new chart. Right-click chart → Style → Save Style → name it "v2 Setups."

      Reference

      How to read this

      Plain-English definitions for everything in Edge Intelligence and Stats Snapshot. The math each metric uses is shown beneath the description.

      Today's read (Home)

      Today's session

      Realized P/L from trades you closed today, plus the count.

      Σ pl(t) where exit_date == today

      Buffer / Trades remaining

      How many more trades you can take before hitting your portfolio risk cap.

      floor((maxRiskPct% of account - openRisk) / nextRisk)

      Sector lean

      Top-rated sectors (Strong Buy / Buy) you should bias toward; Avoid list is sectors graded Sell or Strong Sell. Update on Sundays.

      Last 30-day P/L

      Realized P/L over the rolling window you set in Settings. The kill switch fires when this number is worse than -7% of your account.

      Σ pl(t) where exit_date ≥ today - killSwitchDays

      Career read (Stats)

      Total P/L

      Realized profit or loss across every closed trade in the current filter. Open trades counted separately as Open exposure.

      Σ pl(t) where status != 'open'

      Win rate

      Percentage of closed trades with positive P/L. Doesn't say anything about size of wins or losses — pair with Avg R.

      wins / (wins + losses) × 100

      Avg R / Trade

      Average outcome in units of risk. +1R means the trade made one times the planned risk dollars. +0.5R averaged across many trades is a strong edge.

      R(t) = pl(t) / riskDollars(t)
      avg = mean(R) over closed trades

      R Expectancy

      What an average trade should make you in R, weighted by win rate. Above 0R = profitable system.

      winRate × avgWinR + lossRate × avgLossR

      Profit Factor

      Total dollars won divided by total dollars lost. 1.50+ is good; 2.00+ is rare. Above 1.00 means winners outpace losers in dollar terms.

      Σ wins / |Σ losses|

      Expectancy ($)

      Average dollar result per closed trade. Multiplies trade volume to project monthly P/L.

      Σ pl(t) / count(closed)

      Open exposure

      Estimated dollars at risk across currently-open positions, summed from each trade's logged risk dollars.

      Σ riskDollars(t) where status == 'open'

      Best / Worst setup

      Pattern with the highest / lowest cumulative P/L across all closed trades. Needs ≥ 5 closed trades for the comparison to be meaningful.

      Execution intelligence

      Stock vs options edge

      Compares closed stock trades against closed option trades using P/L, win rate, and average R.

      group by instrument; avgR = mean(pl / riskDollars)

      Spread drag

      Splits intraday option trades by bid/ask spread. Wide spreads show whether market-maker friction is eating the setup.

      spread% = (ask - bid) / mid × 100

      Fill quality

      Compares your entry to the option mid. Paying above mid is execution drag; getting mid or better is clean.

      slippage% = (entry - mid) / mid × 100

      Intraday context intelligence

      Context alignment

      Scores whether setup bias, VWAP confluence, and breadth agree with the trade direction.

      alignment = passing context checks / available checks

      ORB quality

      Separates ORB up/down trades and OR range buckets, then tracks P/L, win rate, avg R, and target-hit rate.

      OR range% = RNG / OR_HI × 100

      VWAP edge

      Groups intraday trades by LONG BIAS, SHORT BIAS, MIXED, or legacy above/below VWAP labels.

      Execution friction score

      Blends spread, fill quality, entry window, context alignment, and trade number of day into Low / Moderate / High friction buckets.

      score = average(pass/fail execution checks) × 100

      Process quality

      Process Quality (Good %)

      How often you followed the plan. Each closed trade is graded Good (followed setup, sizing, exit rules), Okay (one minor drift), or Bad (broke multiple rules).

      Good count / graded count × 100

      Discretionary / thesis-broke exits

      Trades exited on a gut call or because the thesis broke before reaching target or stop. Often a behavioral leak — these tend to net negative.

      Risk controls

      Kill switch

      Auto-stop trigger. Fires when rolling P/L over your kill-switch window is worse than -7% of account. While active, the new-trade flow is blocked. Window is editable in Settings (default 30 days).

      kill = (Σ pl over last N days) / account ≤ -7%

      Risk per trade (regime-based)

      How much of the account is risked on a single trade. Risk-on uses the high tier, Neutral the mid, Risk-off the low. Set in Settings.

      nextRisk = account × regimeRiskPct

      Max positions / Max risk %

      Hard portfolio limits. Max positions caps simultaneously-open trades. Max risk % caps the sum of all open risk as a percent of account.
